This project is dedicated to a literature genre known as cosmic horror.
It purpose is to aid a writer to build a truly lovecraftian world, and to come up with its story.

What's a cosmic horror

Lovecraftian horror, also called cosmic horror or eldritch horror, is a genre, often called a subgenre of horror, fantasy fiction and weird fiction that emphasizes the horror of the unknowable and incomprehensible more than gore or other elements of shock. It is named after American author H. P. Lovecraft (1890â€“1937).

Dreads of popular literature

The Horus Heresy of Warhammer 40k

Warhammer 40,000, a universe known as the stage for everlasting battle between humans and Chaos gods. There are direct references and inspirations to Lovecraftian fiction alike. Forces beyond comprehension actively impact humans in this fiction.
The King in Yellow

Hastur considered to be created by H. P. Lovecraft. Thruth is, it was written by R. W. Chambers, who was influenced by Lovecraft, almost as much as Lovecraft was influenced by him.

Lovecraftian Horror in Games

Bloodborne and Souls-likes

Hidetaka Miyazaki is known for never leaving a chance to make a Lovecraft reference in game design. Bloodborne could be the peak of it all with its Great Ones. Other Souls-likes like Sekiro and Elden Ring also could contain monsters and deaities, whose dreadful nature is presented through difficulty of figthing them.

Terraria

Eye of Chtulhu, Brain of Chtulhu, finally - Chtulhu himself. An imaginary world full of magic is also deadly by nature.

In The Mouth of Madness

The film centers on a horror novelist whose works manifest into reality, blurring the line between fiction and madness, reminiscent of Lovecraft's themes of an uncaring universe. The pervasive sense of paranoia and existential dread reflects Lovecraft's idea that knowledge can lead to insanity, as characters confront horrifying truths.
